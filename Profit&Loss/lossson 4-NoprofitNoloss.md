## Lesson 5 — Always Loss

### Question
Ramesh sold two horses for Rs. 12,000 each. He sold one at 20% profit and the other at 20% loss. What is the total profit or loss (in percent) on the whole transaction?

### Easy explanation
- When two equal-cost items are sold, a % profit on one and the same % loss on the other do not cancel to zero — there is a net loss.
- For small x (here 20%), the net loss% can be computed by the formula: Loss% = x^2 / 100 (when costs are equal). For x = 20, Loss% = 20^2 / 100 = 4%.

### Short calculation (using the formula)
- x = 20 → Loss% = x^2 / 100 = 400 / 100 = 4% loss.

### Calculation by numbers (step-by-step)
- Suppose cost prices are the same for both horses (let CP each = C). He sold both for Rs. 12,000 each, so SP1 = SP2 = 12,000.
- If one was sold at 20% profit, then SP1 = C × 1.20 = 12,000 → C = 12,000 / 1.20 = 10,000.
- If the second was sold at 20% loss, then SP2 = C × 0.80 = 12,000 → C = 12,000 / 0.80 = 15,000.
- These two C values differ because selling prices were same but profit/loss percentages were applied to different cost prices. To get total profit/loss, use actual CP and SP totals:
	- Total CP = 10,000 + 15,000 = 25,000
	- Total SP = 12,000 + 12,000 = 24,000
	- Net loss = Total CP − Total SP = 25,000 − 24,000 = 1,000
	- Loss% = (Net loss / Total CP) × 100 = (1,000 / 25,000) × 100 = 4%

**Answer:** 4% loss on the whole transaction.

---

Tip: For equal selling prices with ±x% on two items, the quick result is a loss of x^2/100. For equal cost prices with opposite ±x% the result differs; be careful which quantities are equal when applying shortcuts.
