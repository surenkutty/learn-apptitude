### Question 1
A man bought pencils at the rate of 6 for Rs. 4 and sold them at the rate of 4 for Rs. 6. What is his gain (in percent)?

### Solution
 
- Cost Price (CP) per pencil = 4 / 6 = 2/3 Rs.
- Selling Price (SP) per pencil = 6 / 4 = 3/2 Rs.
- Profit per pencil = SP - CP = 3/2 - 2/3 = 5/6 Rs.
- Profit% = (Profit / CP) × 100 = ((5/6) / (2/3)) × 100

    Calculation:

    (5/6) × (3/2) × 100 = (15/12) × 100 = 1.25 × 100 = 125%

**Answer:** 125% profit.

### Question 2
If eggs are bought 12 for Rs. 10 and sold 10 for Rs. 12, what is the gain or loss (in percent)?

### Solution

- Cost Price (CP) per egg = 10 / 12 = 5/6 Rs.
- Selling Price (SP) per egg = 12 / 10 = 6/5 Rs.
- Profit per egg = SP - CP = 6/5 - 5/6 = 11/30 Rs.
- Profit% = (Profit / CP) × 100 = ((11/30) / (5/6)) × 100

  Calculation:

  (11/30) × (6/5) × 100 = (66/150) × 100 = (11/25) × 100 = 44%

**Answer:** 44% profit.
