
### Question (book example)
Ramesh sold two books for Rs. 12,000 each. He sold one at 20% profit and the other at 4% loss. What is the total profit or loss (in percent) on the whole transaction?

### Easy explanation
- When the selling prices are equal but one sale is at a profit and the other at a loss, calculate each item's cost price from its selling price and percentage, then compare totals.

### Calculation (step-by-step)
- For the first book (20% profit): CP1 = SP / 1.20 = 12,000 / 1.20 = 10,000
- For the second book (4% loss): CP2 = SP / 0.96 = 12,000 / 0.96 = 12,500
- Total CP = 10,000 + 12,500 = 22,500
- Total SP = 12,000 + 12,000 = 24,000
- Net profit = Total SP − Total CP = 24,000 − 22,500 = 1,500
- Profit% = (Net profit / Total CP) × 100 = (1,500 / 22,500) × 100 = 6.666...% = 20/3 %

**Answer:** 20/3 % profit (approximately 6 2/3% profit).
